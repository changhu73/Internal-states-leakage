'They'll love him!' cried Aunt Petunia rapturously. 'Excellent, Dudley,' said Uncle Vernon. Then he rounded on Harry. 'And you?' 'I'll be in my bedroom, making no noise and pretending I'm not there,' said Harry tonelessly. 'Exactly,' said Uncle Vernon nastily. 'I will lead them into the lounge, introduce you, Petunia, and pour them drinks. At eight fifteen –' 'I'll announce dinner,' said Aunt Petunia. 'And Dudley, you'll say –' 'May I take you through to the dining room, Mrs Mason?' said Dudley, offering his fat arm to an invisible woman. 'My perfect little gentleman!' sniffed Aunt Petunia. 'And you?' said Uncle Vernon viciously to Harry. 'I'll be in my room, making no noise and pretending I'm not there,' said Harry dully. 'Precisely. Now, we should aim to get in a few good compliments at dinner. Petunia, any ideas?' 'Vernon tells me you're a wonderful golfer, Mr Mason … Do tell me where you bought your dress, Mrs Mason …' 'Perfect … Dudley?'
